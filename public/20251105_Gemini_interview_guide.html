Prepare Your Interview

Your quick guide to practicing with AI (Gemini) on your mobile

The Goal for Today

Use technology to practice and gain confidence. The objectives are:

  • Lose the fear. Practice gives you security and calms your nerves.
  • Have a "Personal Tutor." AI (Gemini) is like a friend that helps you practice without judgment.
  • Gain Confidence. When you practice, you realize you know more than you think.

What Do We Need?

Your Mobile Phone

Nothing else is needed.

The Gemini App

It will be our interviewer.

Your Voice

It's easier to speak than to write.

The "Magic Instruction" (Prompt)

A "Prompt" is the **initial command** we give to Gemini. We must tell it to become a kind interviewer who speaks with simple language.

The 3-Step Formula:

The Role: "Act as a **very kind** recruiter using **simple language**."

The Job: "I am interviewing for **[YOUR JOB HERE]**."

The Rules: "Ask me **one question** at a time. When I answer, give me a **simple piece of advice**."

Complete Example (to copy and paste):
"Act as a very kind recruiter using simple language. I am interviewing for a Warehouse Worker position. Ask me one question at a time. When I answer, give me a simple piece of advice."

Let's Practice! Key Questions

Practice 1: Motivation and Drive

AI Question:

"Why do you want this job?" or "Tell me a little about yourself."

Ideas for Responding:

  • Say you are eager to work.
  • Explain that you are responsible.
  • "I need the job and I am a very hard worker."

Practice 2: Punctuality

AI Question:

"Are you a punctual person?" or "What do you do if you are running late?"

Ideas for Responding:

  • The answer is ALWAYS YES.
  • "I am very punctual, I like to arrive 10 minutes early."
  • "If I had a serious problem, I would call immediately."

Practice 3: Conflicts

AI Question:

"What do you do if you have a problem with a co-worker?"

Ideas for Responding:

  • They want to hear that you are a calm person.
  • "I would try to talk it out calmly with the co-worker."
  • "If the problem is serious, I would inform my manager."

What if there's a "Group Dynamic"?

Sometimes they put you in a group to see how you collaborate. They are not looking for the smartest person, but the one who knows how to work in a team.

LISTEN

It's the most important thing. Don't interrupt. Listen to what others say.

PROPOSE

Don't stay silent. Give your idea calmly. (Ex: "I think we could...")

COLLABORATE

Don't give orders ("Do this"). Better to ask ("What do you think if we do...?").

The End: Your Turn to Ask

When they say, "Do you have any questions?", you must **ALWAYS say YES**. It shows interest.

  • Question 1: "What are the usual work hours or shifts for this position?"
  • Question 2: "What does a normal workday look like here?"
  • Question 3: "When do you think I will hear back about the selection process?"

Good luck! Confidence is gained by practicing.
